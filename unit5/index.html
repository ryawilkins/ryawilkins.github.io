How this came about

I wanted one bike that does it all; gravel, road, fast, comfortable. That takes the best of bicycle engineering and technology, with a view to where things are heading. That won't be obsolete. That will ride in rain or sunshine and put a big smile across my face. This is the journey.

Start with requirements

Consider things like:

  • Bottom braket, threaded so there's no creaking, T47 for routing hydro line in BB
  • For a custom frame fit, what material will be used? Carbon, ti, steel? Don't forget budget! What about budded tubing if ti or steel?
  • Discs or rim brakes? What type of riding are you doing?
  • Tyre clearance. The trend is to larger tyres... 28mm 30mm. Remember the tyre will be measured wider on the mounted rim.
  • Electronic or mechanical? Why? Because the frame needs to allow routing and if you want mechanical you need mount points on the frame. Consider riding - if you like wet weather riding, the electric means no cables to get dirty which then impact shifting performance. Shimano or SRAM?
  • Other?

Fit for a frame

So you want a custom frame that does it all; gravel, road, fast, comfortable. So, you need a bike fit!

  • Position of bike handlebars, relative to seat and crank axle

  • How do you know the body position is right. Use the no hands test!
